Document: Arden University Student Complaints Procedure
Institution: Arden University
Document Type: Complaints and Appeals Policy

SECTION: Scope of the Complaints Procedure
This procedure applies to complaints arising from a student’s educational experience,
other than disputes relating to assessment and examinations. Complaints may be
raised in respect of academic and administrative support or other services
provided by Arden University, as well as complaints relating to facilities,
learning resources, alleged harassment by staff, or alleged discrimination.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

SECTION: What the Complaints Procedure Does Not Cover
The procedure does NOT apply to:
- Appeals against academic judgment of examiners
- Disciplinary matters, which are dealt with separately
In such cases, separate procedures exist.:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

SECTION: Principles and Commitment
Arden University commits to:
- Treat complaints seriously and respond to students’ needs
- Provide clear information and support
- Ensure complaints are dealt with confidentially
- Ensure responses are timely and remain impartial
- Ensure complainants are not disadvantaged by raising a complaint:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

SECTION: Early Resolution at Local Stage
Whenever possible, complaints should be resolved informally close to their
origin without formality. This may involve speaking with staff such as a lecturer
or Student Support team. Complaints at this stage can be raised face to face,
by telephone, in writing, or by email.:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

SECTION: Formal Complaint Procedure
If informal resolution is not possible or satisfies the student, they should refer
their complaint to the formal complaint procedure. At this stage, the University
will consider the complaint in more detail, investigate relevant information,
and provide a formal written response.:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

SECTION: Suspension or Termination of Complaints
If a complaint overlaps with other University regulations or policies, advice
should be sought and the progress of the complaint may be suspended. In
exceptional cases, where a complaint is deemed unacceptable, disruptive,
vexatious, or malicious, the University may suspend or terminate the complaint
process and may invoke disciplinary procedures.:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

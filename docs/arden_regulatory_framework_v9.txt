SECTION: Introduction and Purpose
This Regulatory Framework is used for the development and delivery of programmes of study
leading to Arden University awards. It provides a framework for quality assurance, assessment
regulations, progression rules and academic standards governing all programmes of study. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

SECTION: Regulatory Framework Governance
The University operates its programmes and awards its degrees, diplomas and certificates
in accordance with this Regulatory Framework and associated Quality Assurance documents.
The Academic Board has responsibility for oversight of this framework. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

SECTION: Assessment Regulations Overview
The Regulatory Framework includes assessment rules including conduct of assessments,
submission requirements, deferral and extension provisions, and procedures for examinations
which apply to all academic programmes. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

SECTION: Progression and Completion Rules
Progression decisions, level completion and withdrawal regulations define the outcomes
that may be applied following assessment results and the completion of credits at a given level. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

SECTION: Conduct of Assessment
The Framework clarifies rules for conducting assessments, including time-constrained
assessments, multiple choice questions (MCQs), examination conduct, and procedures for
submission, deferral and extension of assessed work. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

SECTION: Award Classification
Regulations describe how awards such as bachelors’ and masters’ degrees are classified,
including calculation of credit and recognition of prior learning. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

SECTION: Definitions and Interpretation
The document includes definitions of terms used in the Regulatory Framework to
ensure consistent interpretation across all regulatory processes. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
